Reading: at h = 1 the bootstrap dominates on per-day CRPS (the body acknowledges
this in Section 5.2). The substantive question R3 RE4 raises is whether the
regime structure of CHMM produces lower CRPS than the bootstrap at multi-day
horizons (h = 5, 20). Negative ΔCRPS at h ∈ {5, 20} would support the body's
'use-case differentiation' framing on a forecast-horizon axis; positive ΔCRPS
at every horizon reads as 'no horizon at which CHMM dominates the bootstrap on
CRPS', which is consistent with the body's 'CHMM beats bootstrap on structural
use cases (regime-conditional VaR, parametric copula composition, parametric
privacy)' rather than on the marginal-distribution axis.
