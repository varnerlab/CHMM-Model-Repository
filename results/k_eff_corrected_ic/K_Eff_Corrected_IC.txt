Method            : refit CHMM-N at each K in [3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21], compute K_eff via the
                    standardized-distance single-linkage diagnostic of
                    run_state_distinctness.jl (Euclidean in z-scored (mu, sigma),
                    tau = 0.2), then re-evaluate AIC/BIC/HQC/CAIC with
                    p_eff = K_eff * (K_eff - 1) + 2 * K_eff in the penalty term.
                    Log-likelihood unchanged from the K_nom fit.

Reading           : if K_eff < K_nom at large K, the parameter-count penalty drops
                    and the IC at the corrected operating point can re-rank.  The
                    expected pattern: AIC, which is least sensitive to p, will be
                    near-monotone in ll; BIC/CAIC, which scale with log(n)*p, will
                    favour higher K under K_eff than under K_nom whenever K_eff
                    is meaningfully below K_nom.
