Reading.
  At each operating point, 'in_CI(%)' is the fraction of simulated IS paths whose
  per-path excess kurtosis falls inside the L=20 bootstrap CI on observed [2.17, 12.40].
  '≤ CI_HI(%)' is the fraction below the upper bound (the relevant one-sided test for
  overshoot, since at this λ the constraint is not undershoot).

  R2 concern: at K*=3 the aggregate sim kurt 14.91 sits ABOVE the upper bound 12.40, so
  the headline heavy-tail row could be statistically distinguishable from observed at α=0.05
  on a one-sided over-shoot test. The per-path 'in_CI(%)' and '≤ CI_HI(%)' columns above
  quantify this directly: if 'in_CI(%)' is well below 50%, the penalised CHMM-t IS kurtosis
  distribution is not centred inside the bootstrap envelope and the headline framing should
  be revised. If 'in_CI(%)' is above 50% even with aggregate kurt above the upper bound,
  the result is a heavy-right-tail-of-paths effect with most of the mass inside the CI.
